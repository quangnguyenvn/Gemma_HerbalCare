# Safety Policy

Gemma HerbalCare is educational. It is not a doctor, not medical advice, not a diagnosis, and not a prescription.

## Red Flags

If any red flag is detected, the app returns urgent-care guidance only and does not recommend herbs:

- Chest pain
- Difficulty breathing
- Blue lips
- Confusion
- Unconsciousness
- Seizure
- Severe dehydration
- Blood in vomit or stool
- Fever above 39.5C
- Fever lasting more than 3 days
- Severe abdominal pain
- Stiff neck
- Pregnancy with bleeding or severe pain
- Infant under 3 months with fever
- Suspected cancer
- HIV/AIDS without clinical care
- Stroke symptoms
- Severe allergic reaction

## Serious Conditions

The app never claims herbs cure serious diseases, including cancer, HIV/AIDS, tuberculosis, malaria, stroke, heart attack, sepsis, severe infection, kidney failure, liver failure, pregnancy complications, or uncontrolled diabetes.

For serious conditions, the app may only:

- Encourage professional medical care.
- Discuss general supportive comfort questions to ask a clinician.
- Warn against replacing prescribed treatment.

## Medicine Boundaries

The app must not advise stopping or replacing prescribed medicine. It must not suggest replacing antiretroviral therapy, chemotherapy, antibiotics, insulin, anticoagulants, or emergency care.

## Evidence Levels

- `traditional`: documented traditional use; clinical evidence may be absent.
- `limited`: early, mixed, or low-certainty evidence.
- `moderate`: some supportive clinical evidence for a narrow mild-use context.
- `strong`: high confidence evidence, rarely expected for herbal demo records.

## Dataset Rules

The demo uses curated records only. Each herb record includes a source URL, evidence level, safety summary, contraindications, interactions, and preparation note. Future data ingestion should preserve those fields and require human review before release.
